About the Team

Birthed in 2013, the Humor Genome Team focuses on processing humor and attempting to analyze humor data. Academic majors of interest include business administration, business management, computational media, computer engineering, computer science, mathematics, and psychology. The current meeting schedule and location is Klaus 1440 on Tuesdays at 1:30 PM - 2:20 PM. This website was developed by the Web-Development Subteam and is separate from the main Web-Development Subteam's website.



Goals

To build an Internet repository of humor, broadly interpreted. Items which are considered funny by various groups would be tracked via social networking integration. The resulting big data would be available for analytics, which, in particular, could include research that will help better understand and perhaps even quantify humor. Possible crowd sourcing of jokes or computer generated humor.



Issues Involved/Addressed

Study of humor, data analytics and machine learning, artificial intelligence, social networks. The team looks for students who have background or interest in web development, web applications, social media, graphics, artificial intelligence, databases (CmpE, CS, CM majors), big data, analytics, machine learning, algorithms (CSE, MATH majors), marketing, commericialization (MGMT majors), digital media, humor, improvisation, storytelling (IAH majors), humor, creativity, and learning (PSYC majors).
